Centenarian - Musical Composition

🎵 Centenarian Musical Project

Part of our Longevity Documentary series, this composition explores themes of wisdom, experience, and the celebration of long life through musical storytelling.

Original Poem by Bryne Tan

Centenarian

Your face—
a canvas etched with time—
holds the quiet weight of wisdom.
Each line tells a tale:
of unspeakable horrors under occupation,
of survival, and the trembling roar
of a Lion Nation being born.

You watched the Lion Cub rise—
stumbling, growing
through uncertain years,
then standing tall with quiet pride.

Your eyes, once lit with youthful fire,
now softened by the passing years,
hold a kaleidoscope of memory:
the clatter of rickshaws,
the pulse of the first MRT,
the skyline that rose
where kampongs once whispered.

You are not merely old—
you are chosen.
A living bridge across generations.

Your story
must be told,
again and again,
until the future learns to listen.

Your children carry your legacy,
woven gently into their DNA,
and their children's children
will echo your strength—
a legacy of quiet fortitude,
etched into the soul
of a nation.

Musical Inspiration

This profound poem captures the essence of Singapore's journey through the eyes of those who lived it all. The composition aims to translate these powerful images into music:

  • The weight of wisdom and lived experience
  • The transformation from kampongs to modern skyline
  • The pulse of progress - from rickshaws to MRT
  • The quiet strength and fortitude of generations
  • The bridge between past and future
  • The living legacy woven into a nation's DNA

Musical Elements: The composition incorporates gentle harmonies, contemplative melodies, and rich orchestrations that reflect the depth and beauty of a century of living.

About Singapore Music SIG

Our Mission

To foster musical creativity, collaboration, and appreciation within the Singapore community. We bring together musicians of all skill levels and musical interests to share knowledge, create together, and celebrate the universal language of music.

What We Do

  • Regular jam sessions and musical gatherings
  • Workshops on various musical genres and techniques
  • Collaborative composition projects
  • Performance opportunities
  • Music appreciation and listening sessions
